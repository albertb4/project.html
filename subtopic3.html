URL https:// www.studiobinder.com/blog/what-is-imax-definition/
Site Author Rafael Abreu
Abstract This source is an article by Rafael Abreu about the history of IMAX technology and how it helped change the way movies are made forever. Abreu first discusses some of the key characteristics of IMAX technology: heavier, noisier camera, horizontal film stock, and higher resolution on the cameras. Abreu then goes into detail about the history of IMAX and how it came to be, starting in 1967 with the first IMAX theater debuting in 1974. IMAX was mostly used for documentary filmmaking until the late 2000s when Christopher Nolan shot multiple scenes of The Dark Knight on IMAX cameras. To this day, IMAX has become a fixture of modern film.

URL https://skokielibrary.info/blog/423/how-technology-changed-the-movies
Site Author Skokie Public Library
Abstract This article goes into detail about certain antiquated and modern technological advancements that helped change the film industry forever. They start by going back to the kinetoscope and cinematograph and then move into the mid 1900s and into the digital age as well. Some of the key advancements the article highlights are the introduction of steadicam, Dolby Stereo technology, and CGI, all of which helped change the way in which films could be seen and heard. The article also mentions how generative artifical intelligence (AI) and streaming services are two modern technological advancements that have both helped and hurt the film industry.
